# Course Evaluation Questionnaire - Quick Reference Guide

## For Students

### How to Complete the Evaluation

1. **Login** to your student account
2. Navigate to **"Course Evaluation"** from the main menu
3. **Select a course** from the list (only active courses can be evaluated)
4. **Rate each statement** using the 5-point scale
5. **Add comments** (optional but encouraged)
6. **Submit** your evaluation

### Rating Scale Guide

| Rating | Meaning | When to Use |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **5** | Strongly Agree | The statement is completely true |
| **4** | Agree | The statement is mostly true |
| **3** | Neutral | Neither agree nor disagree |
| **2** | Disagree | The statement is mostly false |
| **1** | Strongly Disagree | The statement is completely false |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### For Students

**Q: Are my evaluations anonymous?**
A: Yes, your identity is kept confidential when results are shared with instructors.

**Q: Can I change my evaluation after submitting?**
A: No, evaluations are final once submitted. Please review carefully before submitting.

**Q: Do I have to fill out the comments section?**
A: No, it's optional, but detailed feedback helps instructors improve.

**Q: When are evaluations due?**
A: Evaluation periods are announced each semester. Check for deadlines in your student portal.

**Q: Can I evaluate a course I dropped?**
A: No, only courses you completed are available for evaluation.

### For Instructors

**Q: When will I see my results?**
A: Results are typically available 2 weeks after the evaluation period ends.

**Q: Can I see individual student responses?**
A: No, only aggregated data is provided to maintain student anonymity.

**Q: What if I disagree with the feedback?**
A: Discuss concerns with your department head. Focus on constructive patterns rather than individual comments.

**Q: How are evaluations used in performance reviews?**
A: They are one component among many, including peer reviews, self-assessments, and other metrics.

**Q: Can I respond to student comments?**
A: Not directly, but you can address common themes in your next class or improvement plan.

**Q: What defines an "anomaly"?**

A: Yes, use the export feature in the dashboard for reports and analysis.

**Q: How do I customize the questionnaire?**
A: Contact your system administrator. Questions can be modified in the configuration file.

A: Aim for at least 60% participation for statistically significant results.
